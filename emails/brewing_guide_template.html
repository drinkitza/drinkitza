TRADITIONAL BREWING STEPS

1 FILL YOUR GOURD
Fill your mate gourd about 2/3 full with yerba mate leaves. Shake gently to settle the dust.
2 TILT & MOISTEN
Tilt the gourd at a 45° angle and pour a small amount of cool water (never boiling!) to moisten the leaves.
3 INSERT BOMBILLA
Insert the bombilla (metal straw) along the side of the gourd, with the filtered end at the bottom.
4 ADD WARM WATER
Pour warm water (150-170°F, never boiling) into the empty space. Let it steep for a moment.
5 SIP & REFILL
Sip through the bombilla until you hear a gurgling sound, then refill and pass to the next person!

ALTERNATIVE BREWING METHODS

Don't have a traditional gourd and bombilla? No problem! Yerba mate is versatile and can be prepared in several ways:

FRENCH PRESS METHOD
Add 1-2 tablespoons of yerba mate to your French press. Pour hot (not boiling) water and steep for 4-5 minutes before pressing.
TEA INFUSER METHOD
Place yerba mate in a tea infuser or tea bag. Steep in hot water for 3-5 minutes, just like you would with regular tea.
COLD BREW METHOD
Add yerba mate to cold water and refrigerate overnight. Strain and enjoy a refreshing, less bitter version with all the benefits.
